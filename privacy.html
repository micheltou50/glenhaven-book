Privacy Policy

How we collect, use, store and protect your personal information — in line with the Australian Privacy Act 1988 and the Australian Privacy Principles.

Last updated: 22 June 2026

1. About this policy

This Privacy Policy explains how Chaddy Mardini (we, us, our), operator of the short-term rental property Glenhaven in Katoomba, New South Wales, handles your personal information. We are committed to protecting your privacy and complying with the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) (Privacy Act) and the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs).

By using this website or making a booking, you consent to the collection, use and disclosure of your personal information as described in this policy.

2. What personal information we collect

We collect personal information that is reasonably necessary to take and manage your booking and to run our accommodation business. This may include:

  • Identity & contact details — your name, email address and phone number;
  • Booking details — your stay dates, number and type of guests, special requests and any messages you send us;
  • Payment information — payments are processed by Stripe. We receive confirmation of payment and limited details (such as the amount and a transaction reference), but we do not collect or store your full card number;
  • Correspondence — messages you send via our contact form, email or reviews;
  • Technical & usage data — information your browser sends when you visit our website, such as your IP address, device and browser type, and pages viewed.

You do not have to give us your personal information, and you may deal with us anonymously or using a pseudonym where it is lawful and practicable. However, we cannot process a booking without the details needed to confirm and deliver your stay.

3. How we collect it

We collect personal information:

  • directly from you — when you make a booking, fill in our contact or review forms, or email us;
  • through our service providers — for example our payment provider (Stripe) when you pay;
  • from booking platforms — if you booked or enquired via a platform such as Airbnb, we may receive your details from them; and
  • automatically — through standard web server logs and any analytics on our website.

4. Why we collect, hold and use it

We use your personal information to:

  • process, confirm and manage your booking and payment;
  • communicate with you about your stay, including sending confirmations, access codes and pre-arrival and check-out information;
  • respond to your enquiries, requests and complaints;
  • manage cleaning, maintenance and guest support for the Property;
  • keep records and meet our legal, tax and short-term rental regulatory obligations (including the NSW STRA framework);
  • protect our property and legal rights, and prevent fraud or misuse; and
  • where you have agreed, send you occasional marketing about offers and availability (see clause 6).

5. Who we share it with

We do not sell your personal information. We disclose it only as needed to run our business, including to:

6. Direct marketing & your right to opt out

From time to time we may send you marketing messages (for example, a return-guest offer) by email. We will only do so where permitted under the Spam Act 2003 (Cth). Every marketing email includes an easy way to unsubscribe, and you can also opt out at any time by emailing info@stayops.com.au. We will action your request promptly. Transactional messages about an active booking (such as confirmations and access codes) are not marketing and will still be sent.

7. Overseas disclosure

Some of our service providers may store or process information outside Australia. For example, our payment, email and hosting providers may process data in the United States or other countries. Our booking database is hosted in Australia (Supabase, Sydney region). Where we disclose your information to an overseas recipient, we take reasonable steps to ensure it is handled consistently with the APPs.

8. How we store and protect it

We take reasonable steps to protect your personal information from misuse, interference, loss, and unauthorised access, modification or disclosure. These include access-controlled systems, encryption in transit, and limiting access to those who need it. No system is completely secure, but we work to keep your information safe and to respond appropriately to any data breach in line with the Notifiable Data Breaches scheme.

9. How long we keep it

We keep your personal information only for as long as needed for the purposes above, including to meet legal, tax and regulatory record-keeping obligations. When it is no longer needed, we take reasonable steps to destroy or de-identify it.

10. Accessing & correcting your information

You may ask us for access to the personal information we hold about you, and to correct it if it is inaccurate, out of date or incomplete. Contact us using the details in clause 13. We will respond within a reasonable time. There is normally no charge, though we may recover reasonable costs for substantial access requests. If we decline a request, we will explain why.

11. Cookies & website analytics

Our website may use cookies and similar technologies to make the site work, remember your preferences and understand how the site is used. You can set your browser to refuse cookies, although some features may not work as well as a result.

12. Complaints

If you think we have mishandled your personal information or breached the APPs, please contact us first (clause 13) so we can investigate and respond — we aim to resolve complaints within a reasonable time. If you are not satisfied with our response, you may complain to the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC) at www.oaic.gov.au or by calling 1300 363 992.
